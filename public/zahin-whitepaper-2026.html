Executive Summary

The case for sovereign AI

Pakistan has 240 million people — the world's 6th largest population — and zero sovereign AI models. No foundation models trained on Urdu, Punjabi, Sindhi, Pashto, or Balochi. Every AI system serving Pakistan's economy, healthcare, education, and governance is owned, operated, and controlled by foreign entities.

This is not a technology gap. It is a sovereignty crisis. When the models that control a nation's information infrastructure are built, trained, and hosted by foreign powers, that nation's digital future is not its own. Pakistan cannot afford to be a consumer of AI built for others.

Zahin is a non-profit AI lab building Pakistan's first sovereign language models. Starting with fine-tuning Google's Gemma on Pakistani languages, Zahin will prove capability, deploy in critical sectors, and build toward Zahin-1 — Pakistan's own foundation model. The total investment: $25.55 million across three phases, beginning with just $50,000.

This paper presents the crisis, the opportunity, the technical approach, and the financial roadmap. Pakistan has the talent, the demographics, and the strategic imperative. What it lacks is the institution. Zahin is that institution.

The Crisis

The language coverage gap

The most fundamental expression of AI sovereignty is language. A model that cannot understand a nation's languages cannot serve its people. Pakistan's five major languages are among the most underrepresented in global AI systems.

Pakistani Language Coverage in Global AI

478 million speakers across five major Pakistani languages have near-zero representation in global AI systems. These are not minority languages — Urdu alone has more speakers than German, French, and Italian combined.

Every day without sovereign AI capability is a day Pakistan's linguistic heritage is excluded from the systems that will increasingly mediate access to information, healthcare, education, government services, and economic opportunity. The cost of inaction compounds with every model trained without Pakistani language data.

Policy Alignment

From policy to execution

The National AI Policy 2025 is a strong foundation. It correctly identifies the strategic imperatives, the talent requirements, and the infrastructure needs. What it lacks is an execution vehicle — a dedicated institution with the technical expertise, operational independence, and singular focus required to translate policy language into functioning AI systems.

Government ministries are structured for policy oversight, not for training language models. Universities produce research papers, not production-grade AI infrastructure. Private companies optimize for profit, not for linguistic coverage of underserved populations. Pakistan needs an institution purpose-built for this mission — independent enough to move at the speed of technology, transparent enough to earn public trust, and mission-driven enough to serve all of Pakistan's languages, not just the commercially viable ones.

The Gap

The policy exists. The lab doesn't.

Zahin is the execution arm Pakistan's AI strategy needs. A non-profit lab with a clear technical mandate, open-science commitment, and phased financial roadmap — designed to turn the aspirations of the National AI Policy into measurable, deployable sovereign AI capability.

Pakistan's AI policy is not the problem. Execution is. Every month of delay widens the gap between Pakistan and nations that have already invested in sovereign AI. Zahin exists to close that gap.

Technical Strategy

Fine-tuning for
Pakistani languages

Why Gemma

Google's Gemma is an open-weight model with strong multilingual foundations and permissive licensing for fine-tuning and redistribution — exactly what sovereign AI requires. Unlike proprietary models, Gemma can be adapted, deployed locally, and released openly.

Positioning vs Qalb

The Qalb project (arXiv:2601.08141) fine-tuned LLaMA 3.1 8B on 1.84 billion Urdu tokens, achieving a weighted score of 90.34 across seven language tasks — state of the art for Urdu. This validates the fine-tuning approach. However, Qalb covers only Urdu, was developed entirely outside Pakistan, and has no deployment path for production applications. Zahin builds on this proven approach but extends it to all five major Pakistani languages, develops the work within Pakistan, and creates a path from research to real-world deployment.

Data Pipeline
  • 01
    Collection
    Urdu news archives, government documents, literature, social media. For lower-resource languages (Sindhi, Balochi), targeted collection from regional media, religious texts, and community contributions.
  • 02
    Synthetic Augmentation
    Use Google's Gemini API to generate instruction-tuning datasets (question/answer pairs, translations, summaries) across all five languages. Validate with human linguists.
  • 03
    Quality Filtering
    Automated deduplication, language detection, and content quality scoring. Human review for cultural appropriateness and linguistic accuracy.

Technical Strategy

Training and evaluation

  • 04
    Fine-Tuning
    LoRA/QLoRA fine-tuning on consumer hardware (Apple Silicon or cloud GPU). Multiple adaptation passes: base language understanding, instruction following, domain-specific (healthcare, legal, agriculture).
  • 05
    Evaluation
    Benchmark against Qalb's 7-task evaluation suite. Extend benchmarks to Punjabi, Sindhi, Pashto, and Balochi. Human evaluation for fluency, cultural accuracy, and task completion.
Technical Architecture
Base Model
Google Gemma (open-weight). Chosen for multilingual foundation, permissive licensing, and community ecosystem.
Fine-Tuning Method
LoRA / QLoRA for parameter-efficient training. Enables sovereign model development on modest compute budgets.
Data Strategy
Multi-source collection (archives, government, social) augmented by synthetic generation and validated by human linguists.
Evaluation Framework
Qalb's 7-task suite extended to 5 languages. Human evaluation for cultural accuracy. Public benchmark publication.
Deployment Target
Local inference on commodity hardware. Cloud-based API for integration partners. Open-weight release on HuggingFace.
Domain Adaptation
Sector-specific fine-tuning for healthcare, agriculture, legal, and education — Pakistan's highest-impact deployment verticals.

The technical approach is deliberately conservative in Phase 1. Fine-tuning an existing open-weight model is proven, cost-effective, and fast. It allows Zahin to demonstrate capability and publish results before committing to the significantly larger investment required for foundation model training in Phase 3.

Research Commitment

Open by default

Sovereign AI does not mean closed AI. Zahin's commitment to open science is not incidental — it is foundational. Open models, open data, and open research are how sovereign AI serves all of Pakistan, not just those who can afford access.

Open-Weight Models
Every model Zahin builds will be released with open weights. Sovereign AI means the people own it — not a corporation, not a government.
Published Research
All research papers, benchmarks, and findings published at major conferences (ACL, EMNLP, NeurIPS) and on arXiv. Peer-reviewed, reproducible, globally accessible.
Open Datasets
The Pakistani language datasets we curate will be released under permissive licenses for researchers worldwide. Better data for Pakistani languages benefits everyone.
Reproducible Science
Training recipes, hyperparameters, evaluation methodology, and infrastructure specifications documented publicly. If we can't reproduce it, we don't publish it.
Why Open?

Open-weight models create more value than proprietary ones. Falcon's 55 million downloads (as of early 2026) prove that open sovereignty attracts global adoption, research collaboration, and talent. Zahin's open commitment ensures Pakistan's AI work benefits the global community — and that the global community contributes back to Pakistan.

Transparency is not a vulnerability. It is the mechanism through which Zahin earns and maintains the trust of the Pakistani public, the international research community, and the funders who make this work possible.
